# Privacy Policy for Tabby

**Effective Date:** March 5, 2026
**Last Updated:** March 5, 2026

---

## 1. Overview

Tabby ("the Extension") is a Chrome browser extension that provides a spreadsheet and to-do list inside Chrome's side panel. This Privacy Policy explains what data the Extension handles, how it is stored, and your rights as a user.

---

## 2. Data We Collect

**Tabby does not collect any personal data.**

We do not collect, transmit, store on external servers, or share:

- Personally identifiable information (name, email, address, age, ID numbers)
- Health information
- Financial or payment information
- Authentication credentials (passwords, PINs, security questions)
- Personal communications (emails, texts, chat messages)
- Location data (IP address, GPS, region)
- Web history or browsing activity
- User activity (clicks, keystrokes, mouse movement, network monitoring)
- Website content

---

## 3. Local Storage

All data you create in Tabby (tables, rows, columns, cell values, and to-do items) is stored **locally on your device** using Chrome's built-in `chrome.storage.local` API.

- Your data **never leaves your browser**
- Your data is **not sent to any server**
- Your data is **not accessible by us or any third party**

---

## 4. Permissions Justification

Tabby requests the following Chrome permissions:

| Permission | Reason |
|---|---|
| `storage` | To save your tables, columns, rows, and to-do items locally in your browser |
| `sidePanel` | To display the spreadsheet and to-do list in Chrome's side panel alongside any webpage |

No other permissions are requested. No network access is required or used.

---

## 5. Third-Party Services

Tabby does **not** use any third-party services, analytics platforms, advertising networks, or external APIs. No data is shared with any third party under any circumstances.

---

## 6. Data Retention and Deletion

Your data persists in Chrome's local storage until you:

- Manually clear it within the extension
- Clear your browser's local storage / extension data via Chrome settings
- Uninstall the extension (which removes all associated local data)

---

## 7. Children's Privacy

Tabby does not knowingly collect data from anyone, including children under the age of 13. Since no data is collected at all, the Extension is safe for all ages.

---
